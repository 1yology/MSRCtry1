Prevalence of Metabolic Syndrome in Medical Students

Type: Cross-Sectional · Specialty: Internal Medicine

Background: Metabolic syndrome is increasingly prevalent among young adults. This study aims to determine its prevalence and associated risk factors among medical students, who often face high stress and irregular schedules.

Methods: A cross-sectional study was conducted among 500 medical students. Data on anthropometric measurements, blood pressure, fasting glucose, and lipid profiles were collected. The IDF criteria were used for diagnosis.

Results: The overall prevalence of metabolic syndrome was found to be 12.4%. It was significantly higher in males (15.2%) compared to females (9.8%). Low HDL cholesterol was the most common abnormality observed (34%).

Conclusion: A concerning proportion of medical students suffer from metabolic syndrome. Early screening and targeted lifestyle interventions are highly recommended to prevent future cardiovascular complications in this population.
